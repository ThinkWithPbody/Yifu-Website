EPSON ET-2850 Book Printing Instructions

  1. To the left, in Page Thumbnails, select all pages with Ctrl+A and Right Click > Crop Pages.

    1. Change the Top/Bottom/Left/Right Crop Box so that only the docuemnt is in the Crop Box.
    2. Select individual misaligned pages, and move the Crop Box while maintaining same overall size as all pages.

  2. Ctrl+P to Print and select Size > Actual Size. Take note of the actual document size displayed above the document preview.

  3. Change settings back to Size > Fit. Disregard any other settings on this page as they will be overwritten automatically.

  4. Click on Properties at the top of the window, next to the name of your physical printer.

  5. Click on the Printing Preset "Text 2-Sided 2-Up Black" and change the User-Defined Document Size. Alternatively, do the following:

    1. Use one of the default Printing Presets. Use "Document - Standard Quality" in most cases.
    2. Change Document Size to User-Defined. This will open a new dialogue for adding custom document sizes.
      1. Change the Paper Width and Paper Height to the document size you have taken note of in step 2.
      2. Change the Paper Size name accordingly, for example "User-Defined 4.3in*7.3in". Click OK.
    3. At the top of the window, change the tab from Main to More Options.
    4. Change the Output Paper to physical paper size to print in, for example Letter.
      1. Check the box Reduce/Enlarge Document, and choose Fit to Page. Also check the box Center.
    5. Go back to Main tab.
    6. Change Orientation as needed.
    7. Change Color to Black/Greyscale as needed.
    8. Change 2-Sided Printing to Auto (Short-edge binding) for double-sided printing.
      1. Click Print Density and choose the appropriate preset for the document.
    9. Change Multipage to 2-Up for two pages on the same side.
      1. Check again and modify accordingly the Orientation reflected by the icons to the left.

  6. Ensure the Print Preview box located near the bottom is checked.

  7. Click OK to close this window, and Print to generate a preview.

  8. A preview window opens. Check View > Printable Area, or click on the fifth icon at the top. Your document should be maximized within this area.

  9. Click Print if everything looks good.
